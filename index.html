About Me

I am a data-driven professional with a strong foundation in financial services and computer science. My academic experience provided me with a solid understanding of the asset management industry, financial data analytics, computer systems, and computer graphics, while also giving me hands-on exposure to SQL, Python, C++, and full stack web development. I have applied these skills in projects involving full population audit testing, data reconciliation, web scraping with Selenium, ray tracing, reverse engineering, and simple game development.

Academic Lineage

I am a graduate and 'double husky' of Northeastern University, where I earned a Bachelor of Science in Business Administration from September 2017 to May 2021. My coursework included Investments, Fixed-Income Securities, Financial Data Analytics, Corporate Finance, and Business Statistics. From January 2022 to May 2024, I completed a Master of Science in Computer Science. Coursework included Algorithms, Computer Systems, Software Vulnerabilities, and a few Computer Graphics courses taught by Professor Mike Shah. My coursework taught me a range of programming languages and experience working with technologies such as raytracing, reverse engineering, full stack development with RESTful APIs, web scraping, and data analytics among others. During my academic career, I was involved with various on-campus organizations, including the Mascot team, NU Symphony Orchestra, and a fraternity where I served as Secretary.

Professional Work Experience

As an Audit Senior Analyst at Fidelity Investments, I have honed my expertise in both operational and IT general controls, participating in various audits over the firm's asset management, operations, and digital assets groups. I have performed audits on Equity Trading, ETF Operations, Derivatives and Collateral Management, Compliance, the commission uses and soft dollar program, and digital asset custody teams. My favorite audit was of the Counterparty Research and Analytics team, where we reviewed the processes and research performed by credit and quantitative analysts and the risk monitoring team's control environment. My prior experience as a Data Reconciliation Specialist at Financial Recovery Technologies enabled me to master SQL scripting for efficient data handling and perform detailed analyses on trade data, enhancing my ability to identify and resolve data discrepancies. While in school, I developed leadership and marketing skills as the school mascot, successfully completed two co-ops at Eaton Vance and Wayfair, and served in various part-time marketing roles for the Boston Bruins and Boston Red Sox.

Projects

React Web App

This is Driver, a motorsports-themed social media platform. A web app built with React, Node.js, and MongoDB to create a Formula 1-focused social media platform. Makes extensive use of the Ergast API to keep the app up-to-date on the latest race, Driver and Constructor standings, and historic data on every Formula 1 driver. Note, the platform is not active so new users/speeds will not load.

Raytracer Optimization Using Bounding Volume Hierarchy

This Raytracer was built in my Non-interactive Computer Graphics course. Written in C++ and custom math libraries, this project involved implementing a BVH structure to optimize rendering speed for a raytracer built during Noninteractive Computer Graphics.

Procedural Generation of 3D Buildings

Using OpenGL, this project places the user on Mars in Space with a skybox and (poor) lighting techniques. The magic of the project lies in the algorithm used to randomly generate structures on the planet.

2D Game Engine

Developed using SDL 3, the Fortune Engine is a (barebones) framework for creating 2D games, containing classes for players, scenes, and texturing capabilities.
